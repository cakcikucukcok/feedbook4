As she was browsing the internet she came across a site offering privacy on a small island in the Bahamas where there were only seven native bamboo huts with thatched roofs. Most of the island was owned by one multi-millionaire and the concept was to be as close to the aborigine life-style as possible. There was no electricity but ice chests would be provided with ice available at the one and only store on the island. There would no stove; however, there would be a barbecue pit in front of each hut and campfires were allowed. Since there was no electricity, kerosene lanterns would be provided for lighting. The huts were close to the beach where there would be a dock with canoes and room for a small yacht to anchor. Close by was the small store, powered by a generator, that stocked a limited variety of food items and other essentials. The final words on the internet promotion were "be prepared."

Gregory was not about to deny his wife's request to reserve one of these huts after seeing how enthusiastically she described the scene as presented online. She made all the necessary arrangements, including the airlines reservations.
