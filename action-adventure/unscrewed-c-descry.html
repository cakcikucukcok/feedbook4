Annie has a mountain of misinformation to conquer as she stumbles into life and learns to deal with her physical and spiritual self. She is thrust into marriage and parenthood in a time when women can't trust the advice of their mothers-any older women. Her baby boomer generation has been freed by the pill. She must define herself as the first of a new breed of women. Sex is an invasion. Love an obligation. Parenthood a guilt-trip. She fights for her children's education and learns the appalling truth about public education. Annie bares her soul and takes us on a voyage through life's quests. You'll love this book because you have shared personally or as a mate-the same search for meaning.
