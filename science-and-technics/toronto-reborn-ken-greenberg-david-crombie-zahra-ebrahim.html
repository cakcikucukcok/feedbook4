Toronto Reborn offers an incisive view of Toronto's development over the last fifty years. The author, one of the principal figures involved in this decisive period in the city’s evolution, captures how Toronto has truly become a new version of itself.

After arriving in Toronto in 1968 as a young immigrant from the United States, Ken Greenberg, an architect and urban engineer, quickly found himself caught up the unfolding story of his adopted city as a participant/observer during the ensuing decades of dramatic, often inspiring but at times frustrating, change in this extraordinary city.

In Toronto Reborn, the creation of a new Toronto can be seen. The city, grafting itself onto old established roots, building on the old foundations, is experiencing a kind of rebirth — literally arising vertically above its old self.
